Blake Tollison Shelton is a famous American country music singer and television personality. The musical sensation made his debut with his self-titled platinum-certified debut album 'Austin', with its single lead-off spending an impressive five weeks at the number one position on the 'Billboard Hot Country Songs' chart. Blake would receive mainstream attention as a coach on the popular TV series 'The Voice' and eventually cement his legacy as one of the most immensely successful musical artists the world has ever seen.

Blake's steady rise to the top of the entertainment industry is truly awe-inspiring. The country musician's passion and commitment towards his genre of music have seen him secure a host of awards and accolades throughout his illustrious career, with his stand in the entertainment industry lauded by a worldwide audience. His journey to the top exudes unparalleled consistency, with nearly all of his catchy album collections subject to immense critical acclaim.

The Beginning Of Blake Shelton

The famous country singer always had a zeal for becoming a world-renowned musician. By the age of 16, the talented teenager made his presence felt on the bar circuit that garnered him statewide recognition. His tour earned him the prestigious 'Denbo Diamond Award', which is the top award for young and aspiring entertainers in the state of Oklahoma.

Blake's musical career had established its foundation, as two weeks after graduating from his high school in 1994; the budding musician would move to Nashville, Tennessee, to launch his songwriting career. This bold move would prove to be a life-altering event, as Shelton would establish strong relations with several music publication houses and record labels to further his growing music career.

The Journey Ensues

After his shift to Nashville, Shelton did not look back as the up-and-coming country musician would land his first solo recording contract with 'Giant Records'. His music comprised a traditional mix of blue-collar rock songs and country ballads, with his hit single Austin topping the country music charts at the number one spot for five weeks straight.

The talented musician's career began to snowball, as by 2002, Blake had hit the charts with his very own self-titled album released by American entertainment conglomerate Warner Bros. Entertainment. Inc after the dissolution of Giant Records. The album's hit singles, namely All Over Me and Ol Red helped it achieve gold status, with Blake Shelton's presence in the world of entertainment beginning to intensify.

A World Renowned Artist In The Making

Blake's musical career had found its stride with authority, as by 2003, the spirited country musician had released his second studio album, The Dreamer that was also subject to immense critical acclaim. The album's hit single 'The Baby' had secured the number one spot on the country music charts and stayed there for an impressive three weeks.

The catchy album would eventually secure the coveted gold status, with its second and third singles Heavy Liftin and Playboys of the Southwestern Word remaining at the top 50 spots on the country music charts. Blake was undeniably on his way to becoming one of the most popular musical artists in the world, with his global outreach beginning to unfurl.

Raining Albums

By 2004, Blake had released a string of highly successful albums that had garnered him immense fame and fortune. Some of the more popular singles from his album titled Blake Shelton's Barn & Grill comprised Some Beach, Goodbye Time, and Nobody But Me that would assist his critically acclaimed album in securing a gold status.

Along with releasing his rather splendid stack of albums, Blake also released an accompanying video collection titled 'Blake Shelton's Barn & Grill: A Video Collection', which was the country star's debut DVD.

Stepping Into The World Reality Television

2007 was special for Blake, with the release of his album Pure Bs as well the commencement of his television appearances, making it a rather eventful year for the country star. Blake Shelton was now a judge on the popular American reality television singing competition program 'Nashville Star', a career move that helped polish his celebrity status that much more.

The country musician's contribution to television also featured him on the judging panel of the popular reality talent contest 'Clash of the Choirs', with his illustrious career now branching out into several other ventures that would make him a desirable icon in the field of entertainment.

A Milestone Achieved

2009 saw Blake release a full-length album titled Startin' Fires, with the following year proving to be one of the most memorable ones for the beloved country musician. The talented musical artist would release two extended-play albums titled Hillbilly Bone and All About Tonight, with his contribution to music getting the deserved recognition in the form of a variety of prestigious musical accolades in 2010.

Blake Shelton would achieve an important milestone in his illustrious career, as 2010 saw him earn an Academy Country Award, a Country Music Association Award, and even a CMT Music Award, making him one of the most accomplished musicians in the entertainment industry.

Becoming An Established Television Personality

2011 saw Blake Shelton become a judge on the hit TV singing competition 'The Voice', with his brand-new album 'Red River Blue' coming out later that same year.

The critically acclaimed album became his first to secure the Billboard 200 No.1 spot and debut at the top of the chart. The album would also comprise three No.1 singles, namely, Honey Bee, God Gave Me To You, and Drink On It, giving his illustrious music career a nearly unmatched aura of magnificence.

His Continued Dominance In The Entertainment Field

2012 would see Blake's television appearances see a monumental ascend, as he was now signed on to be a judge of the third season of 'The Voice'. The following year saw the release of his eighth album 'Based On A True Story…' and several television appearances with other seasoned professionals of the entertainment industry like Adam Levine, Shakira, and even Usher.

In 2013 the country star would secure two significant CMA honors as well as the title of 'Male Vocalist of the Year' by The Country Music Association. His album 'Based On A True Story…' would also receive the 'Album of the Year' baton, further exemplifying his stand as one of the most accomplished musical artists in the world.

Blake Shelton's success as a musical artist would also see him earn yet another CMA award for Male Vocalist of the Year in 2014, with his album 'Bringing Back The Sunshine' proving to be a hit amongst his loyal country music fan base.

The now established musical sensation would go on to deliver several critically acclaimed albums, with his most recent hit single 'God's Country' from his album 'Fully Loaded: God's Country' earning him the prestigious 2019 CMA Single of the Year award.

Blake Shelton's immense passion for his craft speaks for itself. The renowned country artist has built a musical empire that envelopes passion, hard work, and an unparalleled desire to be at the top of the summit of success. His contribution towards music work emanates a strong connection with his loyal fan base, which has only gotten more significant with time. Blake's music encompasses an element of consistency, with nearly all of his albums receiving widespread recognition that pushes him to perfect his craft further. The musical sensation's popularity has seen him enter the world of television as well, with his career trajectory exuding a sense of versatility in recent times. The musical sensation is here to stay, and one can expect his enormous $100 million fortune to get a tad more extensive in the near future.
