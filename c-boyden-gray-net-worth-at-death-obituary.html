Clayland Gray was an American lawyer and diplomat who served as White House Counsel from 1989 to 1993 and as the United States’ ambassador to the European Union from 2006 to 2007. Clayland Gray (February 6, 1943 – May 21, 2023) passed away. Boyden Gray & Associates LLP, a law practice with offices in Washington, D.C., was founded by him.

Gray worked as Chief Justice Earl Warren’s judicial assistant after graduating. He began working with Wilmer Cutler & Pickering in 1968 and later rose to the position of partner there in 1976. By the end of the 1970s, Gray, who had previously been a Democrat, had moved to the Republican Party in protest of Jimmy Carter’s administration. The European Institute, FreedomWorks, the Atlantic Council, and America Abroad Media all had him on their boards of directors.
