Nhung Kate Movies and TV Shows, Age, Height, Family, Education, Nominations

Publish date: 2024-07-18

Nhung Kate, whose real name is Duong Hong Nhung, is a prominent Vietnamese television and film actress, host, and photo model. Known for her roles in popular horror films such as “Soul Stealer” and “The Maid,” she has made a name for herself in the entertainment industry. Here, we delve into her biography, career, achievements, and personal life.

Biography and Career

1989–08: Biography, Before Fame Nhung Kate was born on May 15, 1989, in Hai Phong City, Vietnam. She later adopted the stage name Nhung Kate, a moniker now familiar to movie enthusiasts and bikers across Vietnam. In 2011, she graduated from Hanoi University of Theater and Cinema, marking the beginning of her journey in the world of entertainment.

2009–12: Early Career In 2009, Nhung Kate kick-started her career by participating in various competitions, achieving significant accolades. She secured the Gold Medal at the 2009 World Environmental Movement Exhibition and the Gold Medal at the 2009 People’s Armed Forces National Amateur Exhibition. Notably, she also clinched the First Prize in the Vietnam Super Star Contest 2009.

2010: “Grandma Doesn’t Eat Pizza” and “Crime Cop: The Perfect Mask” Continuing her rise, 2010 saw her win the First Prize in the inaugural Vietnam Film and Television Actor Contest. Her acting debut occurred in the film “Grandma Doesn’t Eat Pizza,” where she played the role of Thao. Following this, she landed a role in the series “Criminal Police: Perfect Mask,” portraying Tu, a victim targeted by a serial killer.

2013–15: Career Development In 2013, Nhung Kate appeared in the film “Better Man,” directed by Vietnamese-Australian filmmaker Do Khoa. She also starred in “The Dust of Cho Lon” as Trang, although the film was banned from screening. The same year, she gained attention for her role as Nathavon in the movie “The Secret of the Golden Triangle,” directed by Nguyen Duong.

2014–15: “Soul Stealing” Her career reached new heights in 2014 when she delivered a standout performance as Tuyet in “Soul Stealer,” solidifying her status as a notable actress.

2016–Present: National and International Recognition In 2016, Nhung Kate was featured in two films, “Super Thief” and “The Maid.” “The Maid” achieved considerable success, earning her the “Special Award for Acting” at the Los Angeles Film Festival and a nomination for “Best Actress in a Feature Film” at the 20th Vietnam Film Festival.

2017–Present: “Fire Files Part 3: The Corpse Speaks” and “Swapping Fates” Continuing her career in 2017, she played the role of police officer Quynh in the movie “Fire Files Part 3: The Corpse Speaks,” directed by Dung Nghe and shown on SCTV14 channel. In 2018, she appeared in “Swapping Fates” as Ha Linh/Tram Anh, directed by Tran Chi Thanh and shown on VTV1 channel.

Personal Life

Nhung Kate’s personal life has been relatively private. She has been in a long-term relationship with actor Johnny Tri Nguyen. Their love story has spanned several years, and the couple has been together for approximately seven years. Johnny Tri Nguyen, known for his roles in action movies, took a step back from acting to open a dojo, where Nhung Kate has been a constant presence by his side.

Achievements and Awards

Nhung Kate’s talent and dedication have earned her recognition in the industry. She received the following awards and nominations:

    • 2016: Green Star for Best Movie Actress (Nominations)
  • 2017: Los Angeles Film Festival – Special Award for Acting (Winner)
  • 2017: Vietnam Film Festival – Best Lead Actress in a Feature Film (Nominations)

Nhung Kate’s journey from her early career achievements to becoming a celebrated actress in Vietnamese cinema is a testament to her talent and commitment. Her versatility in both movies and television shows has endeared her to audiences, and her future in the entertainment industry looks promising. As she continues to captivate audiences with her performances, fans eagerly anticipate her upcoming projects and contributions to the world of Vietnamese cinema.
