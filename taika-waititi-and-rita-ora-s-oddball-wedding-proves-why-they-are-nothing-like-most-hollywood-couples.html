Taika Waititi And Rita Ora's Oddball Wedding Proves Why They Are Nothing Like Most Hollywood Couples

Publish date: 2024-09-09

Highlights

  • Taika Waititi and Rita Ora's wedding was not as extravagant as many assumed.
  • The wedding was planned in just two weeks.
  • Ora wore a Tom Ford dress and borrowed her mother's pearls.

Most of the time when celebrities get married, the tabloids have a field day. After all, they love to report on stars like Nicolas Cage whose marriage only lasted days or juicy stories like Matt LeBlanc refusing to attend Jennifer Aniston and Brad Pitt's wedding. On top of that, the media loves to report on celebrities who spend a fortune on their nuptials.

When Rita Ora got banned from Gordon Ramsay's restaurant, the media loved reporting on that accurately. However, when Ora and Taika Waititi walked down the aisle, many reports turned out to be wrong. Ora and Waititi later revealed the truth about their oddball wedding.

Inside Taika Waititi And Rita Ora's Wedding

In 2020, Marie Claire put together a list of celebrity weddings that cost a staggering amount of money. Putting aside royal weddings, since that is different, it still is mind-blowing to know how much some of these events have cost.

According to Marie Claire, Michael Jordan's wedding to Yvette Prieto cost $10 million. As did Kim Kardashian and Kris Humphries' nuptials. Marie Claire also reported that Justin Timberlake and Jessica Biel paid $6.5 million for their wedding.

Given those figures, some people may have assumed Taika Waititi and Rita Ora paid millions on their wedding. Failing that, it certainly seems like the celebrity couple must have spent hundreds of thousands at least.

In 2023, Waititi and Ora spoke to Vogue about the day they got married in 2022. Based on what the couple told Vogue, reports and commonly held beliefs about their wedding were wrong. Ora even explained how much fun they had seeing the false reports about their wedding.

After speaking to Ora and Waititi, Vogue was able to report on how plans for their wedding came together. As Vogue's piece described, Waititi and Ora spent very little time putting together the plans for their big day.

"As far as the planning process goes, there wasn’t one: The entire event was dreamed up and executed in just two weeks." Given how quickly their wedding was put together, it makes sense that Ora and Waititi's ceremony was remarkably simple.

Waititi explained to Vogue that he and Ora didn't fly around the world to their wedding destination. “It wasn’t in London or in France like everyone reported. It was in L.A. with a small group of friends.” Ora then went on to clarify exactly where they tied the knot. "At our home."

After Waititi went on to joke about giving out their address, he later explained how few people attended their wedding.

“There were about eight people there—just close friends and family, and parents on Zoom.”

Ora explained Waititi's comments further by naming one of the people who played a key role in their day. “It was really us and my sister, Elena. Elena to the rescue!”

Later during the discussion, Waititi remarked about how relaxed the couple's wedding day was. As a part of Waititi's comments, he further outlined many typical elements of weddings that he and Ora didn't bother with.

“Yeah, it was beautiful. Just having close friends and not having it too big. We didn’t have table settings or any of the stressful things that go along with weddings, and it was nice to just have it super-simple. My daughters were there, and they made everything really fun and easy: I think just because we didn’t have the pressure of having caterers and all of these things, you know, people turning up late, and all of the different moving parts.”

Following up on her husband's comments, Ora revealed that they also didn't have a typical wedding reception. Instead, Waititi and Ora were joined by their guests for a simple dinner party held at the home of their friend Guy Oseary.

“He was so kind to host us at the last minute. We got a group of people together, and we just had a great time!” Vogue's article also revealed that the dinner party had a surprise guest. One of the couple's friends hired an Elvis Presley impersonator for entertainment.

What Did Rita Ora Wear When She Married Taika Waititi?

During Taika Waititi and Rita Ora's conversations with Vogue, details about what she wore to their wedding were revealed. According to Vogue's article, Ora wore a Tom Ford dress and Lorraine Schwartz jewels. Ora explained to Vogue why she chose a Tom Ford wedding dress.

“Tom Ford is one of my favorite designers of all time and favorite humans in general,” Rita says. “He’s now one of my close friends, and I adore and admire him so much. To get married in his dress was a dream come true."

Who are Tom Ford's favorite stars to dress, according to Entertainment Weekly?

Ora went on to reveal how fortunate she was to find the right wedding dress. "And because it wasn’t planned, I didn’t know if the right dress was even going to be in town, and I just took the risk and went to the Tom Ford shop, and they had it perfectly waiting with the veil, in my size, no alterations needed to be done. I mean, it was like it was meant to be, to be honest. And it just made me so happy.”

While Ora wore a designer dress and expensive jewels, she spent absolutely nothing on one of the things she wore. As Ora explained, she wore a meaningful piece she borrowed from her mother.

“I wore my mum’s pearls that she got married in. My sister, Elena, put [the bracelet] on my wrist. It was beautiful. My sister walked me down the aisle, and it was just really simple and blissful and calm and private and fun.”
