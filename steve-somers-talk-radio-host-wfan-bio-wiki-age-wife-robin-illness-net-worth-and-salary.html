Steve Somers Biography and Wiki

Steve Somers “the Schmoozer’ is an accomplished American Talk Radio Host who was born and brought up in San Francisco, California. He is widely known for his work with WFAN (660 AM) which is the most listened to a sports radio station based in New York City.

He joined the WFAN sports radio station team in 1987 during its Inauguration. Prior to joining WFAN, he worked as a Television Sportscast at KOVR in Sacramento, at WXIA-TV in Atlanta, and at KNBC in Los Angeles. While he was working at KNBC, he created and launched a sports talk program at KMPC Radio.

    Steve Somers WFAN

    Steve is widely known for his work with WFAN (660 AM) which is the most listened to sports radio station based in New York City. He joined the WFAN sports radio station team in 1987 during its Inauguration. He began working at WFAN as an Overnight Host and titled himself the ‘Captain Midnight’.

    Later Somers served as a Sports Anchor for WWOR-TV as well as a host of some of its sports programs including “The Sweater and the Schmoozer.” He started his talk radio host career while he was still in high school at KYA Radio in San Francisco.

    Steve delivered the high school sports reports on air. Later, he started hosting a news talk show at KABL Radio in San Francisco after his college days. He introduce talk shows which were rare before and were adapted in the latter days. His story was even highlighted on ‘Time Magazine’ naming him as the youngest talk show host of that time all over the country.

    Somers has worked for NBA’s San Francisco Warriors as a public address announcer. He served as a weekend sports anchor for KPIX in San Francisco since 1970. He earned a San Francisco Press Club Award for his work at KPIX. He has also worked as a Television Sportscast at KOVR in Sacramento, at WXIA-TV in Atlanta, and at KNBC in Los Angeles. While he was working at KNBC, he created and launched a sports talk program at KMPC Radio.
