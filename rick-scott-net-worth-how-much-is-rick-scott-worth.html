The Columbia/HCA Controversy

Rick Scott’s involvement with Columbia/HCA, the healthcare company he co-founded, was not without controversy. In 1997, the company faced multiple investigations by federal agencies, including the FBI and IRS, regarding allegations of fraud and illegal activities.

The investigations uncovered fraudulent billing practices, kickbacks to doctors, and other unlawful activities. Columbia/HCA pleaded guilty to 14 felonies and paid a record-breaking fine of $1.7 billion, one of the largest healthcare fraud settlements in U.S. history.

Despite these controversies, Scott maintained his wealth and continued to build his financial empire.
