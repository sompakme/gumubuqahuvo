Robby Krieger’s Guitar Playing

Robby Krieger’s guitar playing was a key element in defining the distinctive sound of The Doors. With his versatile style, Krieger skillfully incorporated various genres into his playing, including jazz, flamenco, and blues. His ability to seamlessly blend these different musical influences added depth and richness to the band’s compositions, setting them apart from their contemporaries.

Krieger’s guitar techniques were diverse, but one approach that became synonymous with his playing was his use of the bottleneck slide. By sliding a glass or metal tube along the strings, he produced a unique and haunting sound that became a signature element of The Doors’ music. This technique, combined with Ray Manzarek’s mesmerizing keyboard work and John Densmore’s jazz-influenced drumming, resulted in a captivating and unconventional sound that captivated audiences.

“Robby’s guitar playing was truly innovative. He had the ability to channel different musical styles and seamlessly incorporate them into our songs. His skill with the bottleneck slide added a layer of emotion and soulfulness that elevated our music to a whole new level.” – Ray Manzarek

Together, Krieger, Manzarek, and Densmore created a musical synergy that allowed The Doors to push boundaries and explore new sonic territories. Their collaboration paved the way for songs like “Light My Fire,” “Riders on the Storm,” and “Break On Through (To the Other Side)” to become timeless classics in rock music history.
