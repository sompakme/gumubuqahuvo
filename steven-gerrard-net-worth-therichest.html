English footballer, Steven George Gerrard has a net worth of $60 million. He plays for and captains Premier League club Liverpool. He also has 90 caps for the England national team. In January 2012, Gerrard signed a contract that will keep him at Liverpool for the rest of his playing days. The team captain will then take on an ambassadorial role for the club. The new deal is likely to keep his earnings at their previous level of £140,000 a week. Gerrard has won two FA Cups, three League Cups, a Uefa Cup and, in 2005, the Champions League.

In 2005 he came third in the voting for the Ballon d'Or, given to the top player in Europe. He is married to Alex Curran, a fashion columnist for the Mirror who was described by the Times as an English uber-Wag (wives and girlfriends). Gerrard has a lucrative endorsement deal with Adidas which earns him £1m a year, taking his income to £9m a year. Steven Gerrard Promotions saw an increase in net assets from £3.7m to £4m in 2010-11 and he is listed as a member of four tax-efficient limited liability partnerships making money from the film industry.
